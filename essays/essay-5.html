Endings

On "Borrasca" by C.K. Walker

"I'm starting to think the author legitimately does have two wolves inside them, except one is a good writer and one is a 13 year old Wattpad author" ~ sXe_savior, Reddit

C.K. Walker somehow always manages to write the most cringely cinematic and cliche endings no matter how fascinating the world and story is initially, one of the most egregious examples, I believe, being Borrasca V. Though maybe that's what allowed her to become a writer for The Haunting of Hill House. She also always somehow manages to mention rock bands like Guns N' Roses into the sound track of her stories– I don't hate rockbands, just think it's somewhat funny that she plays soundtracks…for writing.

I never really dislike her stories though (however, I may end up finding them amusing rather than scary), because I suck at endings too. Massively. If I'm just saying the same thing as I did in the beginning, then it ends up being redundant. If I try to make a connection to the real world– shout out to my AP essay peeps– it sometimes ends up sounding hyperbolic. My high school AP English teacher once laughed at me after reading a connection I drew between robots in Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep to Post-WWII patriarchy. Gave me the nuance point though!

After kindergarten, my mother moved us from Atlanta, Georgia to L.A. to follow her career development. When I turned seven, I then had to move from L.A. to Farmington, Connecticut. In the last weeks of sixth grade, I'd then move back to L.A., where I finished out sixth grade at a middle school, and then switched schools again to another district. Finished out 7th and 8th grade there, tested into a private high school, and had to uproot myself once again. College happened as well. In applications (grad and job), I love detailing these major life changes to explain why I am above-and-beyond in adaptability and versatility.

After each and every one of those changes, I'd be depressed for weeks on end, one inhale from crying my indescribable aches out– I cried a LOT. I have gained no such defenses or vulnerabilities to endings and change despite how much exposure I've been provided. They are awkward, annoying ultimatums that I just do not understand how to deal with.

Maybe that's how C.K. Walker feels as well; it's just that, with writing, she's able to draw from what she knows, and she knows satisfying, happy-ending cinema, so that's what she writes. Real-life isn't exactly that malleable, so I've decided to just keep moving forward instead. In the past, it always pissed me off when people told me to deal with my anxieties and problems by just moving forward and tackling them head on, but now I kind of get it. I'll no longer have things to end, just a long line of never ending To-Dos.

Aight, that's it. How do I finish this...
